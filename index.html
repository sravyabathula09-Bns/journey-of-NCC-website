National Cadet Corps

Unity and Discipline

National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the youth development organization of India that aims to develop discipline, leadership, character, and a spirit of patriotism among students. It provides young people with opportunities to participate in various training activities such as drills, camps, adventure sports, and social service programs. The NCC was established in 1948 with the objective of creating responsible and capable citizens who can contribute positively to the nation. It operates under the Ministry of Defence and is open to school and college students across India. Through its structured training programs, NCC helps cadets develop qualities like teamwork, courage, self-confidence, and leadership. The organization also plays an important role in motivating youth to join the armed forces and serve the nation.

NCC provides basic military training to school and college students. Cadets participate in camps, social service activities, drill training, and national integration programs.


Training Activities

Drill Training

Drill training is one of the most important activities in the National Cadet Corps. It teaches cadets how to move and act together with discipline, coordination, and precision. Through regular drill practice, cadets learn to follow commands correctly and perform movements in a synchronized manner.NCC, drill is usually practiced during parades, camps, and training sessions under the guidance of instructors and senior cadets. It also prepares cadets for important events like ceremonial parades and national celebrations. Drill training not only improves physical coordination but also builds qualities such as teamwork, leadership, obedience, and respect for authority. It plays a key role in shaping cadets into disciplined and responsible individuals.

Weapon Training

"The only real power comes out of a long rifle." Rifle firing is an integral part of Indian Armed Forces.NCC gives the cadets an opportunity to experience rifle firing. There are four standard rifle firing positions namely lying, sitting, kneeling and standing. Lying is the steadiest of all positions where one lays on the ground to fire. In the sitting position both the arms are supported on the inside of the legs. This is mostly used to fire from a higher place. Standing position is where one stands and fires. It is the most difficult position. Finally in the kneeling position one arm is supported by the leg while firing. Rifles have three major parts, namely Stock, Action and Barrel. The Stock is the handle of the firearm and it helps in holding, aiming and firing a gun. The Action is the part of the rifle where cartridges are loaded and fired.The barrel is the part of the rifle that sends the bullet to the target.

Adventure Camps

Adventure camps are an exciting part of training in the National Cadet Corps. These camps are designed to develop courage, confidence, teamwork, and leadership qualities among cadets. Through various adventurous activities, cadets learn to face challenges, overcome fear, and build a strong and determined personality. In adventure camps, NCC cadets participate in activities such as trekking, rock climbing, parasailing, sailing, mountaineering, and cycling expeditions. These activities help cadets improve their physical fitness, mental strength, and problem-solving abilities. Adventure camps are usually conducted in different parts of the country including mountains, forests, rivers, and coastal areas. These camps also give cadets the opportunity to explore nature, learn survival skills, and develop a spirit of adventure.Adventure camps help NCC cadets develop self-confidence, courage, discipline, and leadership skills. These camps encourage cadets to step out of their comfort zone and learn how to work together as a team in challenging situations.

MY NCC Gallery



Role of NCC in MY journey

I SGT. Naga Sravya Bathula , a proud former cadet of the National Cadet Corps. Being a part of NCC has been one of the most valuable experiences in my life. Through NCC training, I learned discipline, leadership, teamwork, and the importance of responsibility During my time as an NCC cadet, I actively participated in several training activities, camps, and competitions. I successfully achieved the SeregentRank, which was a proud moment in my NCC journey. This rank reflects dedication, leadership qualities, and commitment to NCC values. I participated in many NCC camps and activities including the SNIC (Special National Integration Camp) and the Advanced Leadership Camp in Odisha. These camps helped me meet cadets from different parts of the country and understand the importance of unity, teamwork, and cultural diversity. Apart from camps, I also participated in Best Cadet competitions, cultural programs, and debate competitions, which helped me improve my confidence, communication skills, and leadership abilities.
Joining the National Cadet Corps provides many valuable opportunities for students. NCC training helps in both personal and professional development.
• Development of discipline and leadership qualities
• Improvement in physical fitness and mental strength
• Opportunities to attend national and adventure camps
• Exposure to different cultures and teamwork
• Development of confidence and communication skills
• Opportunities to represent your institution in competitions and events
• Preparation for leadership roles and responsibilities

Career Benefits of NCC :
• Preference and additional marks in defence service recruitment
• Opportunities to join the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force
• Special entry schemes for NCC cadets in defence services
• Benefits in government jobs and competitive exams
• Personality development that helps in interviews and leadership roles
